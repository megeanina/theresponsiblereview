UK FCA Launches Mills Review on AI Impact in Financial Services

UK Medium Impact

The UK Financial Conduct Authority launched the "Mills Review" on January 27, 2026, to investigate the long-term impact of AI on financial services, particularly in the retail sector. The call for input concluded on February 24, 2026, with recommendations expected to be presented to the FCA board in summer 2026. The FCA continues to use its principles-based framework rather than creating AI-specific rules.

NIST Releases Draft Cybersecurity Framework Profile for AI

US Medium Impact

NIST released a preliminary draft of the Cybersecurity Framework Profile for Artificial Intelligence (Cyber AI Profile, NIST IR 8596) in February 2026. This extends the NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 to address AI-specific cybersecurity risks and opportunities, working alongside the AI Risk Management Framework. The draft addresses generative AI risks and supply chain security.

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Critical for EU AI Act Compliance

International Medium Impact

ISO/IEC 42001:2023, the world's first international standard for AI Management Systems, becomes crucial for EU AI Act compliance in 2026. Published in December 2023, this certifiable standard provides a structured framework for organizations to demonstrate systematic compliance with risk management, data governance, technical documentation, and human oversight requirements.
